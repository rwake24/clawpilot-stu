My Laziness, Productized.

Inside one Specialist's three-month experiment with Clawpilot — the desktop AI agent that's quietly absorbed his meeting notes, account prep, and to-do list.

By Robert Wakefield & Jeremiah Gutherie · Specialist Team Unit · Filed Monday morning, before coffee

In the last twelve weeks, an AI agent named Clawpilot logged 69 of my customer meetings, drafted 42 account preps before I asked, and tracked 44 action items across Outlook, Teams, and my Obsidian vault. I clicked "approve" a lot. I did not, by any measure, work harder.

The pitch

One desktop agent. Six jobs.

Meeting notes → Obsidian

Pulls Teams transcripts, drafts the note, tags it, links the account. No copy/paste, no reformatting.

Account prep on demand

State of play, opportunity snapshot, push points, watch-outs — generated in your house format.

Action item tracking

Sweeps Outlook, Teams, and meeting notes. One running list. Write-back when done.

MSX hygiene & scorecards

Risk-bucketed pipeline sweeps and milestone updates without opening Dynamics.

Custom skills

Slash-commands you write once and reuse forever. Heartbeats run them on a schedule.

Connects to your stack

M365, MSX, Obsidian, Power BI, the browser, your filesystem. APIs welcome.

A day in the life

"I could have called them. Why?"

8:00 a.m. The morning heartbeat fires. Clawpilot sweeps Obsidian, Outlook, and Teams. A Teams ping arrives with what's on deck and what's overdue.

9:30 a.m. A customer call sits on the calendar at 10. A prep doc lands in the vault — state of play, open opportunities, a push point grounded in last week's exchange, two watch-outs in red.

10:45 a.m. Meeting wraps. The Teams transcript is auto-pulled into a new Obsidian note, tagged, attendees parsed, wikilinked back to the account page. Three action items appear with owners.

4:30 p.m. End-of-day sweep. New AIs from today's meetings get added to the tracker. MSX hygiene flags surface for tomorrow's prep round.
